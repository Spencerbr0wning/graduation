The college chapter

Four years in review.

01
2022–2023 · Freshman

Fresh tracks

Fresh off the best decision of my life — marrying my beautiful wife McKayla in the summer of 2022 — I walked into freshman year with absolutely zero idea what I was doing. I was framing houses, doing D2D sales, and trying to have fun, all at the same time. UVU taught me a lot, I made good friends, and I had to finally learn how to actually use a computer (honestly, a little embarrassing). A few mental breakdowns along the way, but every bit of it was worth it.

02
2023–2024 · Sophomore

Finding the line

A few things were starting to click, but I still had no idea which direction to go. Then I walked into a marketing class taught by Professor Shill, and it changed everything. He didn't just teach — he pushed us to actually do things, think critically, and challenge the way we saw the world. His line that stuck with me: "Everything in this world has been built by someone as dumb as you." I also spent a lot of that year cheering from the front row at McKayla's dance performances. And yes — I took a dance class with her, which officially makes me a collegiate dancer.

03
2024–2025 · Junior

Building the vision

I went all-in on marketing and entrepreneurship. Took every class I could find and tried starting a few things — most of them flopped, but I got really good at failing fast. Then I started doing Amazon reviews, landed brand deals, and began creating content for myself and for brands. Slowly but surely, something started working. I also got laid off that year, which was terrifying, but honestly the greatest blessing. It was exactly the push I needed to go build something real.

04
2025–2026 · Senior

The summit

Before senior year even started, I applied to the Sandbox program at UVU and got in — and it completely changed my trajectory. Through the program, I connected with a sign company and teamed up with Jack, an old mission friend. We sat down with the company, spotted a massive problem that needed solving, and started Vuely. In its first year, it’s already gaining traction every day. I’ll be building it long after graduation — and through all of it, McKayla and I are still out there having fun and chasing the next adventure.
